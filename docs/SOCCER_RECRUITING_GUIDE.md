# Soccer / Football Recruiting Feature Guide

## Overview

The Soccer Recruiting module is a comprehensive system designed to help Milo manage his soccer recruiting process alongside college applications. All features are fully integrated with the existing application tracker and use the same local storage system.

## Getting Started

### 1. Create Your Player Profile

Navigate to the **Soccer** page from the main menu and click "Create Player Profile".

**What to Include:**
- **Name**: Your full name as it appears on official documents
- **Primary Position**: Your main playing position (e.g., Center Back, Striker)
- **Secondary Positions**: Other positions you can play
- **Physical Stats**: Height and dominant foot
- **Languages**: All languages you speak (valuable for international recruiting)
- **Clubs**: Your current and past club teams
- **Summary**: A brief bio describing your playing style and achievements

**Why It Matters**: Your player profile is automatically included in generated coach emails, making outreach more professional and informative.

### 2. Add Highlight Videos

Create highlight packages that showcase your abilities:

**Video Information:**
- **Player Name & Position**: Identifies who and what position is featured
- **YouTube/Vimeo URLs**: Links to edited highlight reels
- **Full Match Links**: Complete game footage for deeper evaluation
- **Tags**: Organize by season, tournament, or type
- **Video Notes**: Explain what coaches should look for

**Best Practices:**
- Keep highlight reels 3-5 minutes
- Lead with your best plays
- Show variety (offense, defense, transitions)
- Include recent footage
- Tag videos clearly for easy reference

## University Research

### Adding Soccer Programs

For each university you're applying to, go to the university's detail page and click the **Soccer** tab.

**Program Information to Research:**
- **Program Name**: Official team name
- **Division Level**: D1, D2, D3, or Club
- **Conference**: Which conference they compete in
- **Official URLs**: Team website, roster, staff directory
- **International Players**: How many international students on the roster
- **Playing Style**: Tags like "possession", "high press", "counter attack"
- **Recent Record**: Last season's performance and achievements
- **Notes**: Your personal observations from watching games, reading about the program

**Research Tips:**
1. Watch game footage on YouTube
2. Read the team's official news/blog
3. Check roster for player backgrounds similar to yours
4. Note coaching staff changes
5. Track tournament/playoff appearances

### Adding Coaches

Within each program, add coaches you want to contact:

**Essential Information:**
- **Name & Role**: Head Coach, Assistant Coach, Recruiting Coordinator
- **Contact Info**: Email (usually on school website) and phone
- **Background Links**: Bio page, LinkedIn profile

**Research Details:**
- **Alma Mater**: Where they played/studied (great conversation starter)
- **Coaching History**: Previous positions and achievements
- **Playing Background**: Their playing career (if applicable)
- **Recruiting Focus**: What they look for (e.g., "international players", "technical defenders")
- **Favorite Formations**: Tactical preferences
- **Personal Notes**: Specific talking points, connections, or observations

**Where to Find This Info:**
- University athletics website (staff directory)
- LinkedIn profiles
- Team media guides (usually PDFs on athletics sites)
- Press releases and news articles
- Past player testimonials

## Best Practices

### Email Outreach Strategy

**Timing:**
- Start 12-18 months before intended enrollment
- Email during their off-season when possible
- Avoid major tournament/game weeks
- Send early in the week (Tuesday-Thursday)

**Frequency:**
- Initial email
- Follow-up after 2 weeks if no response
- Update email after significant achievements
- Don't contact more than once per month without new information

**Content:**
- Keep initial emails concise (200-300 words)
- Always include highlight video link
- Make it easy for them to respond (ask a specific question)
- Show you've researched their program
- Mention specific connections (coach's background, program style)

## FAQ

**Q: How many coaches should I contact per university?**
A: Start with the head coach and recruiting coordinator. If they respond positively, they'll connect you with relevant assistants.

**Q: What if I don't hear back?**
A: Follow up once after 2 weeks. If still no response, move on but keep the door open - sometimes timing just isn't right.

**Q: Should I contact club teams?**
A: Yes! Club soccer can be a great experience and some club programs are very competitive.

**Q: How do I handle multiple interests?**
A: Be honest but tactful. It's okay to be in conversation with multiple programs - this is expected in recruiting.

**Q: What if a coach responds with bad news?**
A: Thank them for their honesty, ask for feedback, and keep the relationship positive. Situations can change.

**Q: How do I know if I'm at the right level (D1 vs D2 vs D3)?**
A: Research rosters at each level, talk to your club coaches, attend ID camps, and be honest about your skill level.
